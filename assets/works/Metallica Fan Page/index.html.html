Biography

Metallica is one of the most famous and influential heavy metal bands of all time, but they came from very humble beginnings. James Hetfield (Vocals/guitar) and Lars Ulrich (drums) did not share much in common except for a love of the New Wave British Heavy Metal (i.e. Black Sabbath, Iron Maiden, and Motorhead) just to name a few. Their obsession with this new sound of music led to them starting a band and the rest is history. Metallica was formed back in 1981 in San Francisco and put their first album “Kill ‘Em All” in 1983. In 1986, they hit the big stage with the release of their 3rd studio album, “Master of Puppets.” Master of Puppets was a groundbreaking success and brought Metallica to being a household name. Their greatest commercial success came with the release of their self-titled album, Metallica (also known as “The Black Album”). To this day, they are still creating new music and touring.
